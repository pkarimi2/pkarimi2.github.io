AC Electrical Conductivity of Random Composites

Developed a numerical model to evaluate electrical conductivity of random composites. The complex ac impedance of random composites was studied. Real and imaginary parts of the complex conductivity vs. the frequency and the ﬁller fraction will be extracted for three-dimensional systems. The results for frequency-dependent permittivity and conductivity can be used as an input to calculate the shielding effectiveness of polymer nanocomposites for different conditions including thickness, polarization type, and incident angle. Utilized a simulation-based approach to shrink the parameter space and found optimal configurations.
