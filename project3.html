AC Electrical Conductivity of Random Composites

Developed a numerical algorithm to evaluate electrical conductivity of random composites. The complex AC impedance of random composites was simulated. Real and imaginary parts of the complex conductivity vs. the frequency and the ﬁller fraction were extracted for three-dimensional systems. The results for frequency-dependent permittivity and conductivity can be used as input to calculate the shielding effectiveness of polymer nanocomposites under various conditions, including thickness, polarization type, and incident angle. Utilized a simulation-based approach to shrink the parameter space and identify optimal configurations.
