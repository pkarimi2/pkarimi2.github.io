About me

Hello! I'm Pouyan Karimi — an engineer with a Ph.D. in Engineering Mechanics from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, with a minor in Electrical and Computer Engineering. My doctoral research, advised by Prof. Martin Ostoja-Starzewski, focused on multiscale computational modeling of advanced materials and random media using high-performance computing, stochastic methods, and nonlinear finite element analysis. I developed scalable simulation tools to predict structural and physical behavior — including collaborative work with Boeing on industry-scale systems.

I hold a Master’s degree in Structural Engineering from the University of Tehran and a Bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering. My background combines strong theoretical training with hands-on experience in structural analysis, materials modeling, and numerical simulation. I’ve applied these skills to solve complex engineering problems in both academic and industrial settings — working with continuum mechanics, inelastic behavior, and real-world design challenges.

In parallel, I have developed robust software tools in Python and C++, with a focus on performance, reliability, and clean code. I’m experienced in parallel computing (MPI/OpenMP), version control (Git), testing practices, and integrating data-driven methods with physics-based models. My broader interests include software engineering, machine learning, and cloud-based simulation workflows.

I thrive at the intersection of structural mechanics, computational science, and software development — building engineering solutions that are rigorous, scalable, and ready for real-world impact.
