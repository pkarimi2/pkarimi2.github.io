Interpenetrating Phase Composites Bandgaps

Employing numerical algorithms leads to dispersion curves, which reveal the existence of electromagnetic bandgaps at low frequencies and low volume fractions of the conductive phase. This composite is generated from a so-called Schwarz Primitive surface, a member of the triply periodic minimal surfaces family. The Primitive-based composite shows bandgaps for transverse-electric (TE) modes where the widths of the bandgaps vary with the volume fraction of the conductive phase.
