Stochastic characteristics and Second Law violations of atomic fluid in Couette flow

Using Non-equilibrium Molecular Dynamics (NEMD) simulations, we study the statistical properties of an atomic fluid undergoing planar Couette flow, in which particles interact via a Lennard-Jones potential. The bispectral density of the shear stress is used to measure the degree of departure from a Gaussian model and the degree of nonlinearity induced in the system owing to the applied strain rate. More evidence is provided by the probability density function of the shear stress. Observed the Violations of the 2nd law of thermodynamics via the shear stress and identified primary variables of interest as density contrast and shear stress.
