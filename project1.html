Modeling of Electrical Properties of Nanocomposites

The goal of this project is to develop reliable computational algorithms to accurately predict the electrical properties of polymer nanocomposites. The algorithm would optimize and guide the experimental efforts in finding an efficient and cost-effective nanocomposites for electrical applications. Such a study is needed to assess the effect of a wide variety of parameters that impact the thermal, mechanical and electrical properties of polymer nanocomposites.

In addition, shielding effectiveness of carbon fiber composites was modeled for several configurations of inclusions. This was done using high-performance computational resources provided by the Computational Science and Engineering (CSE) program at the University of Illinois with our in-house code. Several three dimensional random realizations of different nanofillers for several volume fractions were generated considering a shell around fillers to model interaction of host matrix and the fillers. The random distribution of fillers is generated using the random sequential addition (RSA) method. The project was funded by the Private Sector Program and the Blue Waters sustained-petascale computing project at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA).
